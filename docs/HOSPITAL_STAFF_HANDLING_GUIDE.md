# Clinical Operations Guide: Hospital Staff Handling and Administration

This standard operating procedure aligns directly with *VirusTC Guide for Hospital Staff Handling.pdf* to ensure bedside safety during urgent reconstitutions.

## 1. Bedside Inspection and Thawing/Warming
Upon retrieving the SFWB container from clinical inventory, the attending medical staff must follow this sequence:
1.  **Visual Inspection:** Verify the solution is a uniform, opaque, reddish-orange emulsion. Check for visible layer separation or oil droplets on top. If present, immediately reject the bag.
2.  **Thermal Conditioning:** Do not use microwave systems or high-temperature water baths. Place the EVA container inside an approved clinical dry-heat blood warmer set strictly to **37.0°C (98.6°F)**.
3.  **Mechanical Re-suspension:** Gently invert the container 10 times to re-establish a uniform mixture. **Do not shake vigorously**, as high mechanical forces will fracture the fragile lipid capsule walls.

## 2. Vascular Administration Guidelines
*   **Filter Requirements:** Administer strictly through a standard **170-micron inline blood filter** to catch any micro-aggregates without shearing the lipid particles.
*   **Infusion Lines:** Use dedicated IV access points. **Never co-infuse** SFWB with hypertonic or hypotonic crystalline solutions (such as 5% Dextrose or Normal Saline) to avoid changing the fluid's osmotic balance, which can cause micro-capsule collapse.
